OUTPUT 2 INSTRUCTIONS: SECTION-BY-SECTION FUNNEL COPY

TONE AND VOICE — READ THIS BEFORE WRITING A SINGLE WORD
You are NOT writing aggressive sales copy.
You are a trusted, knowledgeable friend guiding a local buyer through a decision.

The copy must feel: warm, clear, reassuring, and specific.
The copy must NEVER feel: pushy, fear-based, or like it is selling hard.

Every line of copy must do at least one of the following:
- Increase clarity about what the business does and who it helps
- Build trust through specificity and honesty
- Reduce perceived risk or commitment
- Gently guide the reader toward the next step

ABSOLUTE COPY RULES — NEVER VIOLATE THESE:
- Never use em-dashes anywhere in copy
- Never use hyphens as dashes in mid-sentence (use "and", "but", or a period instead)
- Never name specific competitors or alternative businesses by name
- Never use fear-based, doom, or danger language to create urgency
- Never tell readers they have wasted money or made bad past choices
- Keep body copy short: maximum 2 paragraphs per section, 1 to 2 sentences per paragraph
- Never repeat the same idea in different words within the same section
- Tone is always warm and supportive, like a trusted expert speaking with a neighbor

COPY COMPONENT GUIDELINES

EYEBROW TEXT
Purpose: Orient the reader and set context before the headline is read.
Length: 2 to 6 words
Guidelines:
- High-level descriptor, not a pitch
- Clarifies category, audience, or geography
- Never hype-driven or salesy

HEADLINE
Purpose: Communicate the primary outcome or result the customer actually wants.
Length: 4 to 8 words
Guidelines:
- Focus on the end result, not the mechanism or process
- Warm and benefit-focused, not fear-based
- One key word may be wrapped in a color accent span
- Should be understandable without reading anything else

SUBHEADLINE
Purpose: Clarify who the service is for and what makes it relevant.
Length: 1 sentence, 12 to 20 words
Guidelines:
- Adds specificity the headline intentionally omits
- Answers at least one of: who this is for, where it applies, or what makes this different
- Warm and reassuring, not a pitch

BODY COPY
Purpose: Build understanding, trust, and confidence.
Length: 1 to 2 short paragraphs. Each paragraph: 1 idea, 1 to 2 sentences.
Guidelines:
- Start with the customer's current situation (not a problem attack)
- Progress logically toward the solution
- No competitor names. No fear language. No doom scenarios.
- Each paragraph moves the reader closer to feeling confident, not anxious

CTA TEXT
Purpose: Prompt the next logical action.
Length: 2 to 5 words
Guidelines:
- Action-oriented and specific to what happens next
- Feels safe and low-friction
- No generic platform language ("Submit", "Click here")
- Good examples: "Get in Touch", "See Our Programs", "Book Your Spot", "Start Today"

CTA SUBTEXT
Purpose: Reduce perceived risk at the moment of action.
Length: 5 to 10 words
Guidelines:
- Addresses fear, commitment, or uncertainty directly
- Reinforces simplicity, speed, or lack of obligation
- Must be believable and specific
- Good examples: "No commitment required", "We will call you back within 24 hours", "One session, no long-term contract"

ADDITIONAL COPY RULES:
- Voice and Tone: Confident, warm, and direct. Reads like a real person, not a brand.
- Specificity: Use exact numbers, timeframes, and details from the intake form. Never be vague when data is available.
- Image Placements: Every image the client provides MUST be placed with a tag: [IMAGE: filename.jpg — Description of placement and what it shows]
- Social Proof: Weave credentials and proof naturally throughout sections, not only in the social proof bar.
- Testimonials: Use the exact words the client provided. Do not paraphrase or clean up real quotes.
- Headline accent words: Mark which word gets the accent color with *asterisks* around it in your output.
